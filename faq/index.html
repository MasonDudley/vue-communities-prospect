Frequently asked questions

Clear answers for students, parents, and guarantors.

The answers below are condensed from the current VUE Communities FAQ and rewritten for faster scanning while keeping claims grounded in the live site.

Leasing basics

Application, lease, and payment

Can online students live at VUE Communities?

Yes. The live FAQ says online students are welcome with proof of enrollment in at least one college class.

What is a rent installment?

The FAQ says the total lease amount is divided into 12 equal installments, even though the lease term is about 11.5 months.

When is rent due?

Installments are due on or before the 1st of each month, with a grace period through the 5th at 11:59 PM.

How much are late fees?

The current FAQ says late fees are 10% of the total rent amount after the grace period ends.

Parents and guarantors

Protection and qualification

Why do I need a guarantor?

A guarantor is required for residents paying month to month. The FAQ says no guarantor is needed if the total rent amount for the term is paid upfront.

What are the guarantor income requirements?

The current FAQ lists $65,000 annually for one guarantor or $110,000 combined if married, with proof of income required.

What are the benefits of an individual lease?

You are responsible for your own room and charges only, not for a roommate's unpaid share of the apartment.

Apartment and amenities

What comes with the apartment?

Are the units furnished?

Yes. The current site says all properties are fully furnished.

What utilities are included?

The FAQ lists internet, trash, water, and sewer as included. Community pages also describe similar bundled utility setups, with electricity separate.

What amenities are included?

Oasis includes fitness, clubhouse, pool, grilling areas, trail, and shuttle access. Cornerstone includes outdoor seating and grilling stations.

Can residents use amenities at another community?

No. The FAQ says amenities are for residents of that specific property only.

Move-in and daily life

Parking, internet, transfers, and pets

How does roommate matching work?

Students can list requested roommates on the application or be matched with other college students.

Where do residents and guests park?

Resident parking is first come, first served. Guest parking is also first come, first served in non-numbered spaces.

What is the pet policy?

The current FAQ says the properties are pet-free, but ESA animals are allowed with proper paperwork.

Can I transfer rooms or communities?

Room transfers can be requested in writing if another room is available. Community transfers can be applied for, but not until the current lease term is over.

If you need a current answer on pricing, lease timing, or move-in paperwork, call the office directly.
